**IMAGINE-AI : Integrated Microbiome Analytic Generator of Interaction Network Evolution** 

Ember Morrissey (West Virginia University), Edward Brzostek (West Virginia University), Aldo Romero (West Virginia University), Kirsten Hofmockel (Pacific Northwest National Laboratory) Jennifer Pett-Ridge (Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory) 

Advancing domestic bioenergy production and reducing dependence on energy-intensive synthetic fertilizers are central priorities of DOE’s biotechnology mission. Achieving these goals requires harnessing the untapped abilities of microbial communities. Yet, the field faces a fundamental barrier: genes don’t always translate to what an individual microbes does, and predicting what they do when they are working together in teams is even more challenging. Revolutionary discoveries enabled by harnessing the combined ability of microbial teams are possible, but only if we can meet the challenge of accurately predicting, assembling and managing the combined function of microbial teams. Given advances in rapidly generating large \-omics data streams (i.e., genomics: potential function, transcriptomics: actual function, metabolomics: what is made), the scientific community finds themselves in a quandary: data can be generated far more rapidly than it can be comprehensively analyzed by human research teams. As such, there is a perfect opportunity for artificial intelligence (AI) to leverage large amounts of existing data in order to yield new insights into microbial teams to enhance biotechnology and domestic energy production. We propose to build an **Integrated Microbiome Analytic Generator of Interaction Network Evolution-AI (IMAGINE-AI)**. IMAGINE-AI will be an advanced artificial intelligence AI-enabled workflow and AI model to understand and optimize predictions of what microbes do from their genetic potential when they are alone and in complex multispecies microbial teams in the lab and in the field.   
	The first challenge we propose to tackle with IMAGINE-AI is to identify the optimal composition of microbial teams that enhance the activity of nitrogen fixing bacteria that convert nitrogen gas into nitrogen that plants can use. The ability to understand, predict, and enhance nitrogen fixation by free- living bacteria would revolutionize bioenergy and food crop agriculture in two ways. It would reduce the need for synthetic fertilizers that are energy intensive and enhance yields. Using nitrogen fixation as a test bed, we will meet our first objective to demonstrate an AI advantage in linking genomic potential to what microbes are doing by measuring the speed and accuracy of discrete tasks in the IMAGINE-AI workflow vs. traditional human directed workflows. Here, we will use publicly available data on genomic potential and actual microbial function from single species cultures and microbial team cultures. Concurrent to this work, we will pursue our second objective which will set the stage for Phase II of this project by developing the ability to rapidly produce data on the ability of different microbial team assemblages to enhance nitrogen fixation using AI-informed automated lab equipment. This effort will ultimately generate an AI workflow fully integrated with laboratory automation to accelerate discoveries that allow microbial team optimization for nitrogen fixation as well as be generalizable to other desired microbial functions. Collectively, our development of IMAGINE-AI has the potential to transform the scientific community's ability to manage microbial teams and meet DOE’s mission of enhancing US energy security and dominance.
