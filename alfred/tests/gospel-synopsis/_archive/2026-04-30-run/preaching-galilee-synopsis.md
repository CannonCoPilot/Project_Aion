---

## Synopsis Notes

- Mark sets the departure before dawn; Luke sets it at daybreak — a slight chronological difference.
- Mark names Simon and the disciples as the ones who seek Jesus; Luke refers simply to "the people."
- Both accounts record Jesus' stated purpose: to preach to other towns/cities. Mark has "that I may preach … for therefore came I forth"; Luke has "I must preach … for therefore am I sent."
- Mark's summary (v. 39) includes casting out devils; Luke's (v. 44) focuses solely on preaching.
- Luke omits Mark's detail of the early-morning prayer — a devotional element unique to Mark here.
