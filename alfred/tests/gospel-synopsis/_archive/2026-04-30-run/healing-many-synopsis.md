# Scene S5: Healing Many at Evening

> Mark 1:32–34 || Luke 4:40–41 · KJV

---

| Mark 1:32–34 | Luke 4:40–41 |
|---|---|
| **32** And at even, when the sun did set, they brought unto him all that were diseased, and them that were possessed with devils. | **40** Now when the sun was setting, all they that had any sick with divers diseases brought them unto him; and he laid his hands on every one of them, and healed them. |
| **33** And all the city was gathered together at the door. | *(no direct parallel)* |
| **34** And he healed many that were sick of divers diseases, and cast out many devils; and suffered not the devils to speak, because they knew him. | **41** And devils also came out of many, crying out, and saying, Thou art Christ the Son of God. And he rebuking [them] suffered them not to speak: for they knew that he was Christ. |

---

## Synopsis Notes

- Both accounts share the sunset setting and the bringing of the sick; the language is nearly parallel.
- Mark says "all the city was gathered together at the door" (v. 33) — a vivid crowd detail Luke omits.
- Luke specifies that Jesus "laid his hands on every one of them" (v. 40), emphasizing personal, individual healing; Mark does not describe the method.
- Luke records the demons' confession — "Thou art Christ the Son of God" — more fully than Mark, who says only that the demons "knew him."
- Both accounts record that Jesus silenced the demons; Luke adds the explanatory clause "for they knew that he was Christ."
