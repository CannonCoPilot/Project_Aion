# Scene S4: Healing of Simon's Mother-in-Law

> Mark 1:29–31 || Luke 4:38–39 · KJV

---

| Mark 1:29–31 | Luke 4:38–39 |
|---|---|
| **29** ¶ And forthwith, when they were come out of the synagogue, they entered into the house of Simon and Andrew, with James and John. | **38** And he arose out of the synagogue, and entered into Simon's house. And Simon's wife's mother was taken with a great fever; and they besought him for her. |
| **30** But Simon's wife's mother lay sick of a fever, and anon they tell him of her. | *(included above in v. 38)* |
| **31** And he came and took her by the hand, and lifted her up; and immediately the fever left her, and she ministered unto them. | **39** And he stood over her, and rebuked the fever; and it left her: and immediately she arose and ministered unto them. |

---

## Synopsis Notes

- Mark names four disciples present (Simon, Andrew, James, John); Luke simply says "he entered into Simon's house."
- Mark describes Jesus taking her by the hand and lifting her up; Luke says he "stood over her, and rebuked the fever" — a more authoritative gesture.
- Both accounts note that the fever left immediately and that she arose and ministered to them.
- Luke describes the fever as "a great fever," a detail Mark omits.
