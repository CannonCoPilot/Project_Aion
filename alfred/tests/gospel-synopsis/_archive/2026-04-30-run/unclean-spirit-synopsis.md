## Synopsis Notes

- The crowd's cry of astonishment is strikingly similar in both accounts (vv. 22/32); Mark attributes their amazement to authority, Luke adds "power."
- The demon's speech (vv. 24/34) is nearly verbatim in both accounts.
- Luke's account of the exorcism (v. 35) specifies that the devil "threw him in the midst" and "hurt him not" — detail absent in Mark.
- Mark spreads the exorcism over two verses (25–26); Luke compresses it into one (35).
- Both accounts close with a report of spreading fame throughout the surrounding region.
