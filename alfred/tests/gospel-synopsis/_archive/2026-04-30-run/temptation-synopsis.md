## Synopsis Notes

- Mark compresses the entire temptation into two verses; Luke expands it into a structured three-temptation dialogue.
- Mark alone mentions the wild beasts and the angels' ministry during the forty days.
- Luke alone records the three specific temptations: bread/stones, kingdoms of the world, and the temple pinnacle.
- Both accounts agree on the forty-day duration and the Spirit's leading into the wilderness.
