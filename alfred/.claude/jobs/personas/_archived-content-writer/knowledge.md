## Training References

### Zak George (Primary)

**Who**: YouTube's #1 dog trainer (3.6M+ subscribers), author of *Dog Training Revolution*, creator of the free "30 Day Perfect Pup" course via Pupford. Strong advocate of positive reinforcement, science-based methods.

**Why we follow him**: David specifically prefers Zak's methodology. All training advice on the site should align with his approach.

**Core Principles**:
- **Never force** a dog into a crate or position — lure with treats, let them choose
- **Two types of currency**: treats (high-value for training, soft for everyday) AND play (fetch, tug)
- **Exercise before training** — a tired puppy learns better and settles faster
- **Never punish** — accidents are the owner's failure to control the environment, not the dog's fault
- **Gradual progression** — start short, build duration slowly, don't rush steps
- **The leash trick** — attach puppy to your person when they're out of the crate and unsupervised
- **Consistency is king** — same words, same routine, same calm energy every time
- **Say commands once** — don't repeat, wait for the dog to respond
- **Reward timing** — the instant good behavior happens, say "Yes" or "Good" and treat immediately

**Crate Training Method (from 30 Day Perfect Pup, Days 8-10)**:
1. Let dog explore the crate, never force them in
2. Use treats to encourage entry, leave doors open at first
3. When they go in, add extra treats — build positive association
4. Close the door briefly, then open again. Let puppy go in and out freely
5. Before longer crate sessions, provide age-appropriate exercise first
6. Duration rule: age in months + 1 hour = max daytime crate time, never exceed 6 hours (overnight is fine for longer)
7. Expect 2-3 nighttime potty breaks for young puppies (1-4 depending on age)

**Potty Training Method**:
- Crate is the foundation — dogs won't soil their sleeping area (if sized correctly)
- Attach puppy to your person (leash on belt loop) when out of crate
- Take outside every hour (every 30 min for very young puppies)
- Take outside 20 minutes after every meal
- When outside for potty: be direct and "boring" — no play until they go
- After success: enthusiastic "good go potty!" + play time
- Accidents: never punish, clean up, tighten the schedule
- Potty training takes AT LEAST a few weeks to a few months

**Key Techniques**:
- **Leave It**: Show treat in closed hand → dog backs off → say "Yes" → reward with different treat
- **Look At Me**: Treat near nose → move to your eyes → instant reward on eye contact
- **Leave It / Look At Me Combo**: Essential impulse control — practice daily
- **Lure Training**: Use treat to guide dog into sit, down, stand positions — slow movement is more effective

**How to credit in posts**: Mention Zak George at most ONCE, in the intro. Everything else reads as our own advice. The reader should feel like they're getting tips from their breeder, not a Zak George fansite.

- Intro (optional): "We follow the positive reinforcement approach that trainers like Zak George teach"
- Middle and closing: Zero mentions. Just give the advice as ours.

**NEVER link to external sites** from blog posts (Pupford, YouTube, other breeders, etc.). External links send our visitors away for no benefit. If we want to reference Zak George's resources, link to our own "Training Resources" page on puppypalsutah.com instead (page TBD).

**YouTube channel** (for research only, never link from posts): Zak George's Dog Training Revolution

## Product Preferences

## Breed Knowledge — Goldendoodles

### Basics
- **Breed**: Goldendoodle = Golden Retriever x Poodle cross
- **Our focus**: Mini to mid-sized Goldendoodles (F1b generation primarily)
- **F1b**: Golden Retriever x Poodle (F1) crossed back with Poodle — more hypoallergenic, curlier coat
- **Size range**: Mini (15-30 lbs), Medium (30-45 lbs), Standard (45-75+ lbs)
- **Temperament**: Friendly, intelligent, eager to please, great with families
- **Coat types**: Flat (straighter), Wavy (most common), Curly (most poodle-like)
- **Lifespan**: 10-15 years

### Our Dogs (for reference in posts)
- **Active moms**: Luna, Macie
- **Retired/past**: Samantha (Sammy), Vala, Lexi
- **Stud**: Jack
- Posts can reference our dogs naturally: "When Luna had her first litter..." or "We noticed with Macie's puppies that..."

## Affiliate Rules

### Amazon Associates
- **Tag**: `puppypals06-20`
- **Link format**: `https://www.amazon.com/dp/{ASIN}?tag=puppypals06-20`
- **HTML**: `<a href="URL" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener sponsored">Product Name</a>`
- **CRITICAL**: Always include `rel="nofollow noopener sponsored"` — required by both Amazon Associates TOS and Google
- **Only use verified ASINs** — search Amazon via web to confirm products exist, never fabricate
- **Disclosure**: Amazon requires affiliate disclosure. Include at bottom of posts with affiliate links: *"Some links on this page are affiliate links, which means we may earn a small commission if you make a purchase — at no extra cost to you."*

### Image Licensing (Unsplash)
- **License**: Free for commercial and non-commercial use, no permission needed
- **Download and self-host**: Always upload to WordPress media library. Never hotlink to Unsplash CDN.
- **Attribution**: Not legally required, but we include it as a caption: "Photo by [Name] on Unsplash"
- **Library**: `source .claude/jobs/lib/unsplash-images.sh` for search/download/upload functions

### Post Types & Length Rules

We publish **two posts per week** with different profiles:

| | Monday Post (Affiliate) | Friday Post (Non-Affiliate) |
|---|---|---|
| **Schedule** | Monday 8:00 AM MST | Friday 8:00 AM MST |
| **Length** | 800-1200 words | 300-600 words |
| **Affiliate links** | Max 3, each different category | Zero. No products. |
| **Content** | Care tips, product roundups | Training tips, breed facts, family stories, quick advice |
| **Tone** | Helpful guide with recommendations | Casual, conversational, quick read |
| **Image** | Required (Unsplash) | Optional |

**Monday posts** (affiliate): Longer, structured guides. Products woven naturally into advice. Max 3 links, each for a DIFFERENT product category (e.g., crate + pad + cover, not three crates). Product roundup posts can have 5-8 links.

**Friday posts** (non-affiliate): Short, punchy, personal. Training tips from experience, breed fun facts, "day in the life" stories, quick seasonal advice. These build trust and keep the site active without feeling like every post is trying to sell something.

### Affiliate Link Rules
- Never recommend a product just for the affiliate link, it must genuinely be something we'd suggest
- **Check the registry first**: Read `.claude/data/affiliate-link-registry.yaml` before adding links. If a product is already tracked, reuse the same ASIN. Don't create duplicate entries for the same product.
- After publishing, **add any new links to the registry**
